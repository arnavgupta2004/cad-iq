Minimum wall thickness for injection molded plastic parts is 1.5mm to 4.5mm depending on material
Draft angle for vertical walls in molded or cast parts must be at least 1 degree
Internal corner fillet radius must be minimum 0.5mm to avoid stress concentrations
Hole diameter must be at least equal to the wall thickness of the part
Sharp edges with radius less than 0.3mm on exterior surfaces must be avoided
Rib height should not exceed 3 times the nominal wall thickness
Rib thickness at base should be 50 to 70 percent of nominal wall thickness
Boss outer diameter should be at least 2 times the hole diameter
Minimum thread engagement length must be 1.5 times the thread diameter
Surface finish Ra value must be specified for all mating and sealing surfaces
Tolerance stack-up analysis is mandatory for assemblies with more than 3 mating parts
Undercuts must be avoided in the primary draw direction for molded parts
Parting line location must be defined and must not pass through critical surfaces
Weld lines must not occur in high stress or load bearing regions
Gate location for injection molding must be at the thickest section of the part
Uniform wall thickness must be maintained to avoid sink marks and warpage
Minimum hole depth to diameter ratio must not exceed 4 for blind holes
Chamfer or radius must be added to all leading edges for assembly ease
All brackets must have minimum 2 mounting points with adequate bolt boss design
Material specification and grade must be called out on every component drawing
GD&T symbols must be used for all critical features per ASME Y14.5 standard
Flatness tolerance on mating surfaces must not exceed 0.1mm per 100mm length
Symmetry must be enforced on mirrored features to avoid assembly errors
All fastener holes must have positional tolerance called out using true position GD&T
Minimum clearance between adjacent components in assembly must be 0.5mm
